Recall the conjecture from [[2026-02-24]]: if $\det(B)\ne 1$, then the minimum ratio test gives $\theta^*=0$.

Testing various flat norm instances showed this conjecture to be false! I constructed an instance where in an iteration, $\det(B)=2$, but $\theta^*=1$ and $d$ is integral. In this instance, we had
$$
	d_i = \frac{\det(B(i:-A_j))}{\det(B)} = \begin{cases}
	-2/2=-1 & \text{for }i\in\{167,282\} \\
	0/2=0 & \text{otherwise.}
\end{cases}
$$

This was not a one-off iteration, several iterations had the same $\det(B)=2$.
