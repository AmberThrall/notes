Recall from [[2026-05-12]]:

Let $B_1,B_2,\dots,B_\ell$ be the basis matrices where $|\det B_i|>1$ encountered during simplex method for a given right-hand side vector $b\in\Z^m$. Then we found that each $x_{B_i}=B_i^{-1}b$ is integral if there are integral vectors $z_i\in\Z^{t_i}$ such that
$$
\begin{bmatrix}
	G_{B_1} & -k_1I_{t_1} \\
	G_{B_2} & & -k_2I_{t_2} \\
	\vdots & & & \ddots \\
	G_{B_\ell} & & & & -k_\ell I_{t_\ell}
\end{bmatrix}
\begin{bmatrix}b\\ z_1\\\vdots\\ z_\ell\\\end{bmatrix} = 0,
$$
or $Cw=0$ for short.

Consider then the SNF of $C$:
$$
	U_CCV_C = D = \textup{diag}(d_1,d_2,\dots,d_r,0,\dots,0).
$$
Let $w\in\ker(C)$. Then it follows that
$$
	Cw = U_C^{-1}DV_C^{-1}w =0.
$$
Since $U_C$ is unimodular, this boils down the system
$$
	Dy = 0 \text{ where }y = V_C^{-1}w.
$$
But $D=\textup{diag}(d_1,\dots,d_r,0,\dots,0)$, so $Dy=0$ if and only if 
$$
	y_j = 0 \text{ for }j=1,\dots,r,
$$
i.e.,
$$
	V_C^{-1}w\in\textup{span}\{e_{r+1},\dots,e_{m+\sum t_i}\}.
$$
Therefore,
$$
	\ker_\Z(C) = V_C\cdot\textup{span}_\Z\{e_{r+1},\dots,e_{m+\sum t_i}\}.
$$

Thus, our condition becomes that there exists some $w\in\ker_\Z(C)$ where the first $m$-entries are $b$, i.e.,
$$
	b\in\pi(\ker_\Z(C))
$$
where $\pi:\Z^{m+\sum t_i}\rightarrow\Z^m$ is the projection function onto the first $m$-entries.

Let's instead look at the relation between $b$ and the feasible bases.

Let $K$ be a triangle $[v_1,v_2,v_3]$ and $b=e_{01}+e_{12}$, the chain going partially around the boundary of $K$. There are two cases for constraints:

**Case 1:** $e_{01}\in\textup{supp}(b)$: Then the corresponding constraint in $Ax=b$ is given by
$$
	x_{01}^+ - x_{01}^- + (\partial y^+)_{01} - (\partial y^-)_{01} = 1.
$$
Thus, the basis must contain $x_{01}^+$ or $y_{012}^+$. If it selects $y_{012}^+$, then we get an issue in another constraint:
$$
	x_{02}^+ - x_{02}^- + (\partial y^+)_{02} - (\partial y^-)_{02} = 0.
$$

**Case 2:** $e_{02}\not\in\textup{supp}(b)$: Then the corresponding constraint in $Ax=b$ is given by
$$
	x_{02}^+ - x_{02}^- + (\partial y^+)_{02} - (\partial y^-)_{02} = 0.
$$
If the basis chooses $y_{012}^+$, then we get
$$
	x_{02}^+ - x_{02}^- + (-1) - (\partial y^-)_{02} = 0
$$
which forces the selection of $y_{012}^{-}$ or $x_{02}^+$. But, the simplex method won't select both $y^+$ and $y^-$ at the same time. So it must choose $x_{02}^+$. So the basic variables are $\{x_{02}^+,y_{012}^-,x_?^?\}$.

Similarly, if the basis chooses $y_{012}^-$, then we are forced to take $x_{02}^-$.

Notice that our basis matrix of the form
$$
	B = [\pm e_{\sigma_1} \mid \cdots \mid \pm e_{\sigma_k} \mid \pm\partial_{\tau_1} \mid \cdots \mid \pm\partial_{\tau_{m-k}}].
$$
Upon performing EROs, we get that
$$
	B \sim \begin{bmatrix}
		I_S & \pm\partial_S \\
		0 & \tilde{\partial}_S
\end{bmatrix}
$$
where 
- $I_S$ is an identity matrix corresponding to the identity columns
- $\pm\partial_S$ comes from the boundary matrix consisting of rows covered by $I_S$
- $\tilde{\partial_S}$ is the remaining boundary matrix entries in which $x_i=0$

But further EROs lets us cancel $\pm\partial_S$ which leaves us with
$$
	SNF(B) = SNF(I_S)\oplus SNF(\tilde{\partial}_S) = \textup{diag}(1,\dots,1)\oplus SNF(\tilde{\partial}_S).
$$
Note that $\tilde{\partial}_S$ is simply a submatrix of $\partial$. Since the entries of $\partial$ are of the form $\{-1,0,1\}$, it follows that
$$
	d_1 = \gcd\{\text{all $1\times1$ minors}\} = 1.
$$
Thus,
$$
	SNF(\tilde{\partial}_S) = \textup{diag}(1,d_2,\dots,d_r).
$$

For now we **assume** (with support of numerical tests) that every square submatrix $\partial_S$ of $\partial$ has $|\det\partial_S|=p^r$ for some prime $p$ and $r\in\Z_{\ge0}$. Then it would follow that
$$
	SNF(B) = \textup{diag}(1,\dots,1,k,\dots,k)
$$
where $k$ is a power of $p$.

Unfortunately, the $\R P^2$ also satisfies this result, which implies that the SNF condition is not sufficient for the congruence condition. My assumption is that it has to do with $[b]$ having torsion or not.

The homology class $[b]\in H_q(K;\Z)$ has **torsion of order** $n$ if $b\not\in B_q(K;\Z)$ and
$$
	n\cdot b = \partial c~\text{ for some }c\in C_{q+1}(K;\Z),
$$
i.e., $nb\in B_q(K;\Z)$.

Let's assume that there is a feasible basis $B$ such that $G_Bb\not\equiv0\bmod k_B$, i.e., $x_B = B^{-1}b\not\in\Z^m$. Numerical tests suggest that $k_Bb = \partial d$ for some $d\in C_{q+1}(K;\Z)$. In other words, $[b]$ has torsion of order $k_B$.

**Conjecture:** If there is a feasible basis $B$ such that $G_Bb\not\equiv 0\bmod k_B$, then there is a chain $d\in C_{q+1}(K;\Z)$ such that $k_Bb=\partial d$, i.e., $[b]$ has torsion of order $k_B$.
