I was investigating the notion of torsion in relation to the flat norm LP as suggested by [Bala's conversation with Claude](https://claude.ai/share/f8f11717-f51e-44fc-8d12-18d522499fba) and I believe I came up with a counterexample.

I took a triangulation of $\R P^2$, fattened it up by replacing each triangle of $\R P^2$ with a tetrahedra where the fourth point is given by the barycentric coordinates of the triangle. I then randomly generated input chains until I found one that gave fractional solutions. I set $\lambda=0.05$. I did not use the area of the triangles as weights, I simply set the weights to one.

![[SS_2026-05-05_1778014831.png#invert | center]]

For the triangles ($y$-values) in the output, the yellow triangles belong to the original $\R P^2$ and have a coefficient of $\pm 0.5$. The orange triangles are protruding out of the $\R P^2$ along the created tetrahedra and have a coefficient of $\pm 1$.
