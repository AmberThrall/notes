# Exploration of SNF

At any feasible basis, $B$ is of the form
$$
	B = \begin{bmatrix}
		\pm e_{i_1} & \cdots & \pm e_{i_j} & \pm\partial_{i_{j+1}} & \cdots & \pm\partial_{i_m}
	\end{bmatrix}
$$
where each $e_{i_k}$ is a standard basis vector. Since order does not matter for basic variables, we assume WLOG that 
$$
	i_1 < i_2 < \cdots < i_j.
$$
Perform the following row swap operations
$$
	R_{i_k} \leftrightarrow R_k~\text{ }~\forall k=1,\dots,j
$$
and
$$
	R_k \leftarrow -R_k~\text { }~\forall k\text{ where $e_{i_k}$ is negative}
$$
at this point
$$
	UBV = \begin{bmatrix} I_j & \partial_T \\ 0 & \partial_B \end{bmatrix}
$$
where $V=I$ and $U=DR_\pi$ where
$$
	D_{k,\ell} = \begin{cases}
		0 & k\ne\ell \\
		-1 & k=\ell,\text{ and $e_{i_k}$ is negative} \\
		1 & \text{otherwise}
	\end{cases}
$$
and $R_\pi$ is the permutation matrix corresponding to
$$
	\pi: \begin{pmatrix}
		1 & 2 & \cdots & j & j+1 & \cdots & m \\
		i_1 & i_2 & \cdots & i_j & j+1 & \cdots & m
\end{pmatrix}
$$
We then cancel $\partial_T$ with column operations, affecting $V$ which we are not interested in. This leaves us with
$$
	(DR_\pi)BV = \begin{bmatrix} I_j & 0 \\ 0 & \partial' \end{bmatrix}.
$$
The remaining operations only affect, $\partial'$. Let $U'$ and $V'$ such that $U'\partial'V'=SNF(\partial')$. Then we get
$$
	\begin{bmatrix} I_j & 0 \\ 0 & U'\end{bmatrix}(DR_\pi)BV'\begin{bmatrix} I_j & 0 \\ 0 & V'\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} I_j & 0 \\ 0 & SNF(\partial') \end{bmatrix}.
$$
Note here that $|\det U|=|\det U'|$ where $U$ is the matrices to the left of $B$. Note that $D$ and $R_\pi$ only affect the first $j$ columns. So
$$
\begin{bmatrix} I_j & 0 \\ 0 & U'\end{bmatrix}(DR_\pi) = \begin{bmatrix}
	D_jR_j & 0 \\
	0 & U'
\end{bmatrix}
$$
where $D_j$ and $R_j$ are the top left $j\times j$ submatrices of $D$ and $R_\pi$, respectively.


**Therefore,** our conditions only depend on $\partial'$, the $(m-j)\times(m-j)$ submatrix of $[\partial]$.
